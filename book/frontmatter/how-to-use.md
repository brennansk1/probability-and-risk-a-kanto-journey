# How to Use This Book

Welcome, Trainer. This is a complete, self-contained course in probability — built to take you from the very beginning of college math to a **9 or 10 on SOA/CAS Exam P** — told as the story of your journey across the Kanto region. You play **Ash**: every concept is something you must learn to win a battle, save a town, or price a risk, and the Pokédex's *Actuary Mode* poses the problems.

> **Our two promises to you.**
> 1. *You will never need an outside source to understand a concept in this book.* Every idea is taught from the ground up — no step is assumed "obvious."
> 2. *You will never be forced to read what you already know.* Every chapter and every concept opens with a short **test-out gate**; pass it and skip ahead.

## Who this book is for

A motivated reader starting from **arithmetic and the willingness to think**. We do *not* assume fluent algebra, comfort with summation or integral signs, set theory, or any probability vocabulary. Chapters 0–2 (the on-ramp) teach the notation and the calculus the exam silently assumes; if you already own those, the gates let you clear them in minutes.

## How each chapter teaches (the nine-beat arc)

Concepts aren't *summarized* at you — they're *taught*. Every important idea is built the same way:

1. a one-sentence plain idea (before any symbol),
2. a concrete numeric instance from Ash's journey,
3. reasoning it through in words,
4. surfacing and dismantling the tempting wrong idea,
5. translating into notation **one symbol at a time** (named and read aloud),
6. deriving the formula from the instance,
7. ramping the difficulty (simple → twist → general → edge),
8. a picture or table,
9. consolidating into a **Pokédex Entry** (the summary) + a **Recognition Cue** (the exam-speed trigger).

## The panels (what each colored box means)

| Panel | What it is |

| **Pokédex Entry** | The boxed theory/formula you keep — definition, meaning, recognition cue. |

Math is always set in clean type on a calm background — never over art. If you covered every sprite, map, and badge with your hand, you could still learn the math perfectly. *(Symbols you don't recognize yet? See **Chapter 0 → "Reading the Symbols."**)*

## The exam you're training for

- **3 hours · 30 multiple-choice questions (A–E) · computer-based · scored 0–10 (6 passes).**
- **No penalty for wrong answers — never leave a blank.**
- A standard-normal table is provided on-screen (Appendix C teaches you to lean on it less).
- Calculator: **TI-30XS MultiView** (recommended) or **BA II Plus**, memory cleared (Appendix D).
- Topic weights: **General Probability 23–30% · Univariate 44–50% · Multivariate 23–30%.**

## How to study this book to actually pass

1. **Read actively, pencil in hand.** Work each worked example yourself before reading the solution.
2. **Honor the gates.** Take every test-out gate honestly — skip what you own, slow down where you hesitate. The exam rewards what you can *do* under time, not what you recognize.
3. **Do the problems.** Each chapter's **Gym Challenge** is tiered: Route Trainers (mechanics) → Gym Battles (true exam difficulty) → Elite Challenge (integrative). Work them timed at **~6 minutes each**, then check the full Answer Key.
4. **Drill volume in parallel.** This book is your *teacher* and ~300 worked problems; pair it with a question bank (e.g., Coaching Actuaries / ADAPT) for the thousands of timed reps that build speed. Use the **error-log method** in Chapter 15: categorize every miss by root cause and re-test it.
5. **Take the three mock exams** (Chapter 15) under real 3-hour conditions near the end.
6. **Don't sit until you clear the readiness gates:** **24/30** on practice mocks, Coaching Actuaries **Earned Level 7+**, and steady **6-minutes-per-question** pacing.
