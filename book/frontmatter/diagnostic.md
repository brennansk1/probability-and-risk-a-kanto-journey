# The Skip Diagnostic — Test Out of Any Chapter

This book serves two readers at once, and you get to choose which one you are **for each chapter and each concept** — no guessing, no wasted time.

- **The Novice** reads straight through and learns every idea from scratch.
- **The Reviewer** *tests out*: at the top of each chapter, and again at each concept, there's a short gate. Clear it and skip ahead; miss it and the full teaching is right there waiting.

## How to use a chapter-level gate

Think you already know a chapter? **Don't read it — fight it.** Go straight to that chapter's **Gym Challenge** problem set:

1. Work it **timed**, about **6 minutes per problem**, no notes.
2. Check your answers against the full **Answer Key**.
3. Score the chapter's stated mastery bar — typically **80%+ with the correct method, not just the right number**.

Clear the bar → **skip to the next chapter.** Miss it → the chapter is waiting, and now you know exactly which ideas you were missing.

## How to use a concept-level gate

Inside each chapter, every concept opens with a green **"Do you already own this?"** panel — a single quick question with the answer hidden one line below. If you can produce the answer *with the right reasoning* in under a minute, skip to the next concept. If you hesitate, read the lesson; the hesitation **is** the signal.

## Why "fight it, don't read it"

The exam does not reward what you *recognize* on the page — only what you can **do**, correctly, under a ticking clock. A timed problem set measures exactly that. Reading a chapter you half-know feels productive and changes nothing on exam day; failing its problem set tells you precisely where to spend your hours.

## When a gate sends you back

Each chapter ends with a **Badge Earned** checklist — a short list of "you can now, unaided, …" statements tied one-to-one to the exam's learning outcomes. If any item fails, follow its **gym-rematch** pointer back to the exact section to revisit. A badge you can't defend isn't earned yet.

Use the gates honestly and this book costs you only the time you actually need — and not one page more.
