# 🗓️ Your 12-Week Battle Plan — Pallet Town to the Indigo Plateau {.type-normal}

This plan takes you from your first night in Pallet Town to the Indigo Plateau in **twelve weeks**, sitting **Exam P on Friday, September 11, 2026**. It assumes the cadence you set: **five sessions of about 90 minutes a week — roughly 7.5 hours, ~90 focused hours over the course.** That is enough to pass *if the hours are the right kind of hours*: not re-reading, but **retrieving** — working the drills closed-book, taking timed mocks, and clearing a running list of your own mistakes. This schedule is engineered to make that happen, and to keep the show, the story, and the syllabus moving as one.

**It rides on the *Watch Alongside* guide.** Every week names the Indigo League episodes for its chapters — watch them first, read second, drill third. The journey and the exam advance on the same calendar.

> **Exam P, at a glance.** 30 questions · 3 hours · computer-based at a Prometric centre · five-option multiple choice · your **TI-30XS MultiView** on the desk. That is **6 minutes per question** — a pace this plan builds into your hands by mid-course. By the SOA's weighting, **single-variable random variables (Acts I–II) and multivariate random variables (Act III) are each ~40–47% of the exam**, with general probability ~10–17%. So the plan front-loads foundations but spends its heaviest review and every mock on the continuous and multivariate material, where the points live.

## The shape of a week (your five sessions)

Each teaching week covers **two chapters**, A and B, in a fixed rhythm. Keep a **Miss Log** from day one — one line per wrong problem (its ID and the *reason* you missed it). It becomes your final-week study guide.

| # | Session (≈90 min) | What you do |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Chapter A — learn** | Watch A's episode (~22 min). Read the chapter: cold-open → concepts → worked examples. Do the 60-second retrieval grid closed-book. |
| 2 | **Chapter A — drill** | Work A's **Route-Trainer** and **Gym-Battle** problems in the workbook. Log every miss. Redo each miss once, from scratch. |
| 3 | **Chapter B — learn** | Watch B's episode. Read the chapter the same way. |
| 4 | **Chapter B — drill** | Work B's Route-Trainer + Gym-Battle problems. Log misses; redo. |
| 5 | **Mixed review** | Redo this week's misses from memory. Do each chapter's **audit (Team Rocket)** and **rival-trap (Gary)** problems — they are the exam's favourite traps. Then 10 minutes of *last* week's ideas, cold. Stretch into the optional **Elite-Challenge** problems only if time remains. |

In the mock weeks (10–11) the sessions convert: a **full mock needs one unbroken ~3-hour block** — combine two sessions or use a weekend morning — and the sessions on either side are spent reviewing *every* question, right or wrong.

## The final stretch (weeks 10–12) — where the exam is won

- **Mocks under real conditions.** Closed book, formula sheet **from memory** (rebuild Appendix A on blank paper first), the full 3-hour clock, the TI-30XS only. The three mocks in **ch19** are weight-matched to the real exam (general / univariate / multivariate ≈ 8 / 14 / 8 of 30).
- **Review beats re-taking.** After each mock, spend *more* time reviewing than you spent taking it. For every question — even the ones you got right — write the one sentence that names the skill. Wrong ones go in the Miss Log.
- **Remediation is targeted, not broad.** In week 11, your Miss Log will cluster. Pick your **two weakest topics** and reteach them from the chapter, then redrill — don't re-skim everything.
- **Taper.** Cramming the night before lowers scores. Weeks of spaced retrieval are why the two checkpoints exist. The last two days: re-read your formula sheet, redo a handful of Gym-Battle problems you know cold for confidence, sleep. Arrive early; bring two **memory-cleared** TI-30XS units and your IDs (see **Appendix E — Exam Day**).

## Seven rules that make the hours count

1. **Watch first, read second, drill third.** The episode primes the scenario the math then formalises.
2. **Retrieve, don't re-read.** The drills and the cover-the-answer grids are the engine. Closed-book or it doesn't count.
3. **Honour the spacing.** Session 5 each week and the two checkpoints (ch07, ch18) are deliberate spaced review — the single highest-yield habit. Never skip them to "get ahead."
4. **Build the 6-minute pace.** From week 6 on, time your Gym-Battle problems at ~6 minutes. Speed is a trained skill, not a gift.
5. **Keep the Miss Log religiously.** One line per miss, with the *reason*. Weeks 11–12 are simply "empty the log."
6. **Calculator on autopilot.** Rehearse 1-Var Stats, `nCr`, and `eˣ` until they're muscle memory (ch00, **Appendix D**). Fumbling keys is lost points.
7. **Simulate the real thing.** Every mock is a dress rehearsal: Prometric conditions, no notes, 3 hours, then a thorough review.

## If you fall behind

Life happens; the plan flexes. Protect these in order:

- **Never cut:** reading every chapter once · every **Gym-Battle (boss)** problem · both **checkpoints** · all **three mocks** · clearing the **Miss Log** · the **taper**.
- **Cut first, if you must:** the optional **Elite-Challenge** stretch problems, then repeated Route-Trainer reps on topics you already pass.
- **Where to spend recovered time:** Acts II–III (continuous + multivariate). They are ~85% of the exam — if a week slips, slip it out of Act I review (Checkpoint A already backs you up), never out of the mocks.
