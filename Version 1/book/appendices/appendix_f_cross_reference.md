# Appendix F — Cross-Reference to ACTEX/Broverman & Finan + SOA Samples {.type-normal}

This book is a complete, self-contained course — but it is not the only resource you should drill. Two free or widely-owned references are the standard companions for Exam P, and the SOA publishes an official set of sample questions and solutions. This appendix is the bridge: for each chapter it tells you **which sections of the standard manuals cover the same material** and **which SOA sample questions to practice for the outcomes that chapter discharges**. Work a concept here, then immediately reinforce it on the official problem bank using the pointers below.

::: trainers-tip
**HOW TO USE THIS TABLE**

After finishing a chapter and its Gym Challenge problem set, open the SOA sample-question PDF and work the question numbers listed for that chapter's outcomes. The SOA solutions are terse; if a step is opaque, come back to the chapter's worked examples for the *why*. The manual sections are the place to find *additional* drill problems beyond this book's set if you want more volume on a weak topic.
:::

## F.1 — The reference sources

- **ACTEX P Manual (Broverman).** The classic Exam P study manual, organized by syllabus topic. Section numbering follows the manual's own contents; the *topic names* below resolve to it across editions even as section numbers shift between printings.
- **A Probability Course for the Actuaries (Finan).** Marcel Finan's free study guide (open PDF), organized as numbered **Sections** of self-contained lessons with exercises. The section bands cited below are stable across its editions.
- **SOA Exam P Sample Questions & Solutions.** The official two-PDF set (questions + solutions) the SOA maintains for Exam P. It is the single most representative practice source. Question *ranges* below are guidance to the topic clusters within that set; always work from the current edition you download, since the SOA periodically adds and renumbers items.

::: team-rocket
**TRANSMISSION INTERCEPTED — "I'll just memorize the sample-question answers."**

Team Rocket grabbed the SOA solution key and memorized which letter went with which question number. On exam day the numbers were different and the letters meant nothing. **The sample set trains pattern-recognition, not answer-recall.** Work each one cold, check the *method* against the solution, and tag the archetype — that is what transfers.
:::

## F.2 — Topic-weight map (orient before you drill)

| SOA Topic | Weight | This book's chapters |
|---|---|---|
| **1 — General Probability** | 23–30% | Ch 3 (outcomes/axioms), Ch 4 (counting), Ch 5 (conditional, independence, total probability, Bayes) |
| **2 — Univariate Random Variables** | 44–50% | Ch 6 (RV language), Ch 7 (expectation/variance/MGF), Ch 8 (discrete distributions), Ch 9 (continuous & transformations), Ch 10 (insurance loss/payment) |
| **3 — Multivariate Random Variables** | 23–30% | Ch 11 (joint/marginal/conditional), Ch 12 (conditional & double expectation), Ch 13 (covariance & sums), Ch 14 (order statistics & CLT) |

Drill in proportion to weight: the Univariate block (Chs 6–10) is roughly half the exam and deserves roughly half your practice volume.

::: pokedex-entry
**POKÉDEX ENTRY — Reading the outcome codes**

The SOA labels each learning outcome `Topic(letter)`, e.g. **2(e)**. This book carries the same codes in each chapter's front-matter `outcomes:` field and in its Badge Earned checklist, so a code is traceable end to end: outcome **2(e)** → Chapter 10's loss-and-payment lessons → Chapter 10's problems → the deductible/limit cluster of the SOA sample set.

*Recognition cue:* to audit your own readiness for any single outcome, pull its code, find its row above, and confirm you can solve that chapter's problems *and* the matching SOA sample questions unaided.
:::

## F.4 — Coverage guarantee

Per the production plan, every SOA outcome is backed by **at least eight problems** in this book, and every in-scope distribution by **at least six**. The table above is therefore a *reinforcement* map, not a gap-filler: you can pass on this book alone, and the manuals plus the SOA sample set are how you build volume and exam-realistic variety on top of it.

*Manual section numbers are guidance and may shift between editions of the ACTEX/Broverman and Finan texts; the SOA sample-question set is periodically revised and renumbered. Always work from the current edition you have in hand, and reconfirm topic coverage against the official SOA Exam P syllabus (2026).*
